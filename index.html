Research Areas

Our interdisciplinary research focuses on understanding application behaviors and their implications

01

Permission Analysis

Systematic study of application permission requests and their correlation with stated functionality. We analyze patterns in permission usage across different application categories.

02

Data Transmission Studies

Research into data transmission patterns, network communication behaviors, and information flow between applications and external services.

03

User Behavior Tracking

Analysis of tracking mechanisms, behavioral data collection methods, and the construction of user profiles through application interactions.

04

Economic Models

Examination of monetization strategies, subscription models, and economic incentives in application ecosystems.

05

Network Communication

Study of network protocols, data transmission methods, and communication patterns in application architectures.

06

Algorithmic Influence

Research into how algorithms influence user behavior, decision-making patterns, and information consumption in digital environments.

About the Institute

The Applied Science Research Institute is an independent, non-profit research organization dedicated to advancing scientific understanding of application behaviors in digital environments. Our mission is to conduct rigorous, evidence-based research that contributes to knowledge in the fields of software behavior analysis, data science, and human-computer interaction.

Through systematic analysis and peer-reviewed research, we document application behaviors, data collection practices, and interaction patterns. Our work supports informed decision-making by users, developers, and policymakers through transparent and accessible research findings.

We believe in open science, data transparency, and the importance of evidence-based understanding in an increasingly digital world. Our research is conducted with academic rigor and made available to the broader research community.
